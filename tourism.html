Zürich

The largest city in Switzerland having a population of around 402,762 (as of 2016), Zürich is located in the north on the tip of Lake Zürich. This location allows for some beautiful views and tourist activities with a large majority of Switzerland tourism based here. There is a large amount of culture within Zürich with many museums and art galleries all throughout the city, including the Swiss National Museum and the Kunsthaus.

Matterhorn

There are a wide variety of different attractions to be discovered around the Matterhorn. A top excursion is Gornergrat. On this three-thousand metre mountain ridge there is a viewing platform with a truly unforgettable view of the Matterhorn and the surrounding mountain world. Our if you prefer, the Matterhorn can also be admired from the Rothorn. The Matterhorn glacier paradise is the highest summer ski region in Europe and is open 365 days a year.

Interlaken

Today it makes daredevils scream with adrenaline-loaded activities. Straddling the glacier-fed Lakes Thun and Brienz the town is the gateway to Switzerland's fabled Jungfrau region and the country's hottest adventure destination bar none. If the touristy town itself leaves you cold, the mountains on its doorstep will blow your mind, particularly if you’re abseiling waterfalls, thrashing white water or gliding soundlessly above 4000m summits.

Chateau de Chilion

This island castle based on Lake Geneva dates back to 1005 and has always been of great interest to tourists/visitors. Among the most visited castles in Europe you can quickly see why it brings such attention to this area with its stunning surroundings and photo opportunities. The castle can be viewed from a variety of areas and has many secrets to be explored deeper by taking a tour or simply walking around.
